About me

I am a senior student at Brock University, St. Catharines, ON, Canada, working towards my BSc. (Honours) in Computer Science and Economics with a concentration in Software Engineering. My interests include all things tech, but I am not limited as my passions are widespread, also being an avid musician and coffee enthusiast.

I have a passion for everything I do, both in my personal and professional life. Work is not something I consider a chore, but rather a challenge where I strive to exceed my own personal best, developing skills and an intricate social network along the way.


Personal Projects & Work Experience

Cross Border Housing

Winner of multiple entrepreneurial competitions, such as Monster Pitch, Cross Border Housing is a startup from St. Catharines, ON, that is a platform for all international or non-local students, providing listings of local student properties for rent to ease the transition into a Canadian university lifestyle. Beyond housing, a surplus of additional amenities are offered to give the user the smallest amount of work possible in their travels.

My work with CBH is as a web developer, and I will be focusing on both front and back end optimization using PHP with Laravel 5. Check out more here.

CS Breakdown

CS Breakdown is an educational series on topics in Computer Science ranging from advanced algorithm design to detailed mathematical proofs. The series aims to provide high quality and understandable instruction on the selected topics to benefit future Computer Science students.

Similar to Relic, this application also began as a collaboration for course work, but quickly grew beyond the classroom. It is hoped that soon we will have videos targeting people of all skill levels. Read more, or watch a video at the CS Breakdown Homepage.

GP Portfolio

Genetic Programming Portfolio (GPP) is my undergraduate thesis supervised by Professor Brian Ross from the department of Computer Science, and with Professor Ivan Medovikov from the department of Economics at Brock University.

The code implementation uses Java with ECJ, a genetic programming library. The problem will be approached like many symbolic regression problems have been in the past, but analyzed purely from an economic perspective.

Formulize

Through UCOSP (Undergraduate Capstone Open Source Projects) I am working with a team of students at other Canadian universities to contribute to Formulize, an open source data management tool aimed at providing industry level database frameworks to those without the technical skills to do it themselves.

Formulize is written in PHP and is available as a standalone installation, or a module to be attached to your pre-existing website. The development environment follows "git flow" using continuous integration with Travis-CI as well as automated testing with Selenium Builder.

Relic

Relic is a mobile application that provides Brock University students a platform for buying and selling textbooks easily. It makes use of Android and iOS platforms, as well as a central MySQL database using PHP and JSON to communicate with the user's device.

While originally for a capstone project course, myself and the associated developers have continued to pursue the idea to see how it may benefit local students. Read more here, or see below for a commercial outlining the application.

Chess with AI

As part of the final project for Introduction to Artificial Intelligence, myself and classmate Karim Hamasni created a chess engine that used multiple play strategies with alpha-beta pruning to best evaluate it's next move. The entire engine and GUI were written in C#, and the game provides a large variety of options for the user. This includes difficulty selection and playback of previous games and moves to study the decisions made.

ACM ICPC

The ACM-ICPC is an international, team-based programming competition, wherein a team composed of three has use of a single computer and is tasked with solving challenging programming problems under a time restraint. Brock was able to send 2 teams for the 2014/2015 event. All members received an honourable mention, and the Brock Badgers placed among the top teams for the East Central North American event.
