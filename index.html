About me

Personal Projects

Relic

Relic is a mobile application that provides Brock University students a platform for buying and selling textbooks easily. It makes use of Android and iOS platforms, as well as a central MySQL database using PHP and JSON to communicate with the user's device.

While originally for a capstone project course, myself and the associated developers have continued to pursue the idea to see how it may benefit local students. Read more here, or see below for a commercial outlining the application.

CS Breakdown

CS Breakdown is an educational series on topics in Computer Science ranging from advanced algorithm design to detailed mathematical proofs. The series aims to provide high quality and understandable instruction on the selected topics to benefit future Computer Science students.

Similar to Relic, this application also began as a collaboration for course work, but quickly grew beyond the classroom. It is hoped that soon we will have videos targeting people of all skill levels. Read more, or watch a video at the CS Breakdown Homepage.

Cross Border Housing

Winner of Monster Pitch and other entrepreneurial competitions, CBH is a platform for all international or non-local students that provides listings of local student properties for rent to ease the transition into a Canadian university lifestyle. Beyond housing, a surplus of additional amenities are offered to give the user the smallest amount of work possible.

My work with CBH is as a web developer, and I will be focusing on both front and back end optimization for launch this summer. Check out more here.

ACM ICPC

The ACM-ICPC is an international, team-based programming competition, wherein a team composed of three has use of a single computer and is tasked with solving challenging programming problems under a time restraint. Brock was able to send 2 teams for the 2014/2015 event. All members received an honourable mention, and the Brock Badgers placed among the top teams for the East Central North American event.
